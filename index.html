Vaccine Mandate Decision Aid Tool

Explore and compare vaccine mandate scenarios based on public preferences in Australia, France and Italy.

Vaccine mandates can differ in their scope and conditions. This decision aid tool employs discrete choice experiment estimates—guided by targeted literature (e.g. Betsch et al., 2018; Larson et al., 2014)—to illustrate the trade‐offs between key policy attributes. The tool is designed to be self‐explanatory for policy makers.

  • Instructions – How to navigate the tool.
  • Attributes – Detailed descriptions of each mandate feature (Reference level shown in brackets).
  • Inputs – Configure your scenario by selecting alternative levels (the Reference is applied by default).
  • MRS – View marginal rates of substitution (trade‐offs) among attributes.
  • Predicted Vaccine Mandate Uptake – See the predicted acceptance rate and estimated number of participants (base = 2000 per country).
  • Costs & Benefits – Analyse detailed cost components and benefits, with dynamic calculations.
  • Saved Scenarios – Compare and export your saved configurations (CSV download available).

How to Use This Tool

  1. Review the Attributes tab to understand each mandate feature. Hover over the info icons for further details.
  2. In the Inputs tab, select your country and choose an alternative level for each attribute if you wish to depart from the Reference (which is displayed in brackets and is not selectable).
  3. Decide whether to Adjust for Cost of Living? (yes/no) as cost‐of‐living differs across Australia, France and Italy.
  4. Click “Calculate & View Results” to see the predicted uptake rate and the dynamically computed estimated participants (out of 2000).
  5. Examine the MRS tab to view intuitive trade‐off measures between key attributes.
  6. In the Predicted Vaccine Mandate Uptake tab, click “Show Uptake Chart” to display the acceptance rate along with detailed policy recommendations (a popup will appear).
  7. Visit the Costs & Benefits tab to review detailed cards explaining cost components and benefit calculations. Select a benefit scenario (low, medium or high) to update calculations dynamically.
  8. Save your scenarios in the Saved Scenarios tab, compare them, and download the data as CSV.

Key Mandate Attributes

Reference: High‑risk occupations only
Only high‐risk roles (e.g. healthcare, border protection) are required.

Scope of the Mandate (Reference: High‑risk occupations only)

Alternative: All occupations and public spaces – mandates apply to everyone in workplaces and public venues.

Reference: 50 cases/100k with 10% weekly increase
Moderate strain on healthcare resources.

Infection Threshold (Reference: 50 cases/100k, 10% increase)

Alternatives:
– 100 cases/100k with 15% weekly increase: Significantly higher strain.
– 200 cases/100k with 20% weekly increase: Severe strain.

Reference: Low vaccine coverage (50%)
50% vaccinated is the baseline.

Vaccine Coverage Requirement (Reference: 50%)

Alternatives:
– Moderate (70%): Improved protection against outbreaks.
– High (90%): Enables safe lifting of mandates.

Reference: None
No incentives are provided by default.

Incentives for Vaccination (Reference: None)

Alternatives:
– Paid time off (1–3 days): Offers leave for vaccination.
– Government subsidy/discount: Provides a 10% discount on select services.

Reference: Medical exemptions only
Only medically verified exemptions apply.

Exemption Policy (Reference: Medical exemptions only)

Alternatives:
– Medical and religious exemptions.
– Medical, religious and broad personal belief exemptions.

Marginal Rate of Substitution (MRS) Among Attributes

The MRS quantifies the trade‐offs between attributes. For instance, it indicates how much improvement in vaccine coverage is needed to offset a less favourable infection threshold.

Costs & Benefits Analysis

This section details the cost components and benefits of the vaccine mandate scenario. Costs comprise fixed expenditures (administration, enforcement, communication, legal and monitoring costs) and variable costs per compliant participant (e.g. time lost, additional testing, and operational adjustments). Benefits are estimated from reduced healthcare costs and improved public health outcomes. Calculations adjust according to country‐specific cost‐of‐living and benefit scenario (low, medium or high).
